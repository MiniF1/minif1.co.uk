Comments (1) Sunday, 10th April, 2005, 23:42 - These things move slowly, don't they?

A few things to report on the house-buying process. I had a small hiccup with the mortgage, in that a technical problem meant that my application was received after the rates went up. However, my mortgage advisor is negotiating cashback to the value of the rate uplift because he says he applied for it before the rates went up, so fingers crossed that will work out.

I have just received the results of the survey/valuation (I went for the basic one) which say that the house is worth what I'm offering to pay for it and that they don't see any reason why the mortgage lender shouldn't lend me the money for it. Which is good! :) The only things it did note was that the house had experienced some movement, but that there is no evidence to suggest this is ongoing, and that it has been subject to draininage alterations and repair, and suggests that I should check that all necessary permissions and consents were obtained (whatever that means?).

My solicitors have also taken money off me to begin the searches, so I assume that's underway. And that's about all I have to report with regard to the house for the time being.

What else has been going on... Dad ran a 12-car event for the CSMA West Middlesex car club on Friday night which went very well. Steven & Uncle Michael were Course Opening Car while Mum & I were Course Closing Car, which meant we had to keep stopping to pick up code boards and close the marshal controls (although most of them were already closed by the time we got there). Fortuntely the bar was still open when we got back to the Finish, although it was a close call!

After clearing up and getting back to the parents' at about 1am, I then had to get up at 7am to drive back down to Southampton on Saturday morning, as we had a situation at work that needed to be resolved. Me? Going in to work on a Saturday morning? What is the world coming to!

We finally finished at about 12.30pm, at which time I had to rush across to Portsmouth as I was entered in the Portsmouth & Havant Badminton Tournament. I played with Nigel, and after some close games (and some not so close) we finished joint 4th in our group (out of 6).

And as if I haven't played enough badminton recently, I filled in for Wildern's Southampton A team this afternoon but we lost 5-3 to Notorious. However, the result may be subject to change as we may be lodging a protest because one of the girls in the Notorious team played against us for another team earlier in the season (funnily enough in one of the other games I filled in for). But we're not sure what the league rules say on the matter.

So that's me for the moment - work, buying a house, badminton & ralying about covers it. If anything else exciting happens, I'll keep you posted!

Comments (3) Friday, 18th March, 2005, 00:37 - Grown Up

I know I've been quiet for a while on here, but that's because I've been busy. Busy at work, but more importantly busy house-hunting. I've decided it's time to join the world of grown-ups and buy a house I'm bored of renting, having to fit in with other people, pay money out and not get an awful lot back for it, and not be able to hang my own pictures or posters on the walls and personalise my room, for fear of losing a deposit.

So I've been hunting now for about a month, I've looked at numerous houses in and around Southampton, and met lots of lovely people and some rather sad ones. Of all the houses I've looked at (probably ~20), 5 of them were up for sale because the couple who had bought it were splitting up. How sad is that? That's at least a quarter of the houses I saw that were for sale. No wonder there are so many new 1 & 2 bed houses and flats being built at the moment, to cater for all these newly single people.

Anyway, the good news is that I had an offer accepted on Tuesday! It's for a 3-bed semi in Sholing, a suburb of Southampton that's about halfway between work and the centre of Southampton. I'm trying not to let myself get too excited about it, in case it falls through (I've already lost out on 2 houses by being outbid, and the offer on this one is below the asking price so I can't rule out being gsumped). But I've already started planning what's going where...

It will need some work doing, so any volunteers for a bit of DIY? Come on, I know you all probably know more about home maintenance than me... hehe, that's not difficult! But that's what Dad's are for, isn't it?

Comments (0) Friday, 4th February, 2005, 12:09 - Prague

Last weekend the intrepid threesome of myself, Helen & Anna went off to investigate the delights of the capital of the Czech Republic, the wonderful city of Prague. We had a lovely, albeit rather cold (top temp of -4oC!) weekend, visiting all the sights (castle, churches, synagogues, Staropramen brewery...), having snowball fights, and testing the local cuisine.

I'd recommend Prague (Praha to the locals) as a destination (50p a pint!), although not necessarily at this time of year... Having said that, it was fairly quiet on the tourist front which was nice, but we got the impression that it's jam packed in the summer.

This was the first major outing for my new camera, and in 4 days I managed to take 123 pictures! I love digital cameras... Unfortunately, I got back and tried to upload them here for all to see, only to discover that I have run out of space in my 100MB web server allowance So I'm afraid you'll have to wait a bit longer until I can figure out where I can host them, and the stack of other photos I haven't got around to displaying yet.

Watch this space...

Comments (2) Saturday, 8th January, 2005, 12:47 - Christmas all over again

Christmas has come again for me as my best present arrived this week - my parents gave me some money towards a digital SLR camera, so I jumped straight on the net and spent the wonga on one of these little babies It's beautiful, a fantastic combination of my old 35mm SLR and my current Powershot A70 digital compact camera. I have the ability to frame shots again, and don't lose any quality when I zoom in, especially in low light, and if I don't like the picture, I simply erase it! Hence the reason I was able to take about 50 shots of my bedroom the other night on lots of different settings, view them instantly, then get rid of them! Pure magic

It did take me a while to decide which one to buy, it was a tough choice between the Canon 300D and the Nikon D70, two very good cameras. After much deliberation and surfing the net for reviews, sample pictures etc., I finally decided that the Nikon wasn't worth the extra money to me and I am now the happy owner of my 3rd Canon camera.

So I expect you will be seeing plenty of photos up here soon as I will definitely be playing with it a lot!
