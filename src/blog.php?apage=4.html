Comments (1) Wednesday, 15th June, 2005, 23:24 - Settling in

I just realised I haven't written anything on here for a while, as I've been so caught up with the house! Things are coming along nicely. I've got this week off work, so Dad has been helping me attack the first room. Monday saw us strip the wallpaper and polyfilla a few holes, plus we mowed the wilderness and turned it into something resembling a lawn (a very yellow and strawlike lawn, but it's getting there!). Yesterday I sanded the polyfilla down, and today Dad was back again to help hang the wallpaper. We've managed to get the whole room done, it's looking bigger and brighter already! Phase 3 is on Friday, when Mum, Grandma and Siobhan are coming down to help me paint the room and also do some more on the garden (which will hopefully include doing something drastic with the eucalytpus tree which is growing far too fast and threatening to take over). Not bad for a week methinks!

I now have a bed, a single one which was kindly donated to me by Yvette from badminton (between her and Barrie I have gained a lot of stuff, I think their houses must be pretty empty!) so I'm sleeping in relative comfort. I'm still missing a sofa, but it's difficult to find one I like that's small enough to fit in the bay window. But with Marion & Neil's kind houswarming present (4 folding garden chairs), at least I have seating capability now! It's all coming together...

Thanks to all those who came to the housewarming party, it was lovely to see you all, even if I did spend most of the time giving guests the Grand Tour! Thanks also for the lovely e-mails and cards from those who couldn't make it.

Comments (1) Wednesday, 1st June, 2005, 10:14 - I'm in!

Guess what - I'm a homeowner! I am officially in grown-up territory now I got the keys and moved in yesterday, and spent the first night in my very own home last night! I love it, it's fantastic I have a garden, a shed, a loft, a drive, and a hefty debt (but I'm glossing over that for the time being) and it's great! I totally recommend it.

I'm very lucky, as the house was let out before it was put up for sale, and a lot of stuff was therefore left behind, including a fridge, freezer, cooker (gas, hoorah!), washing machine, ironing board, curtains, lights, lampshades (although most of those will be going as soon as I can replace them, especially the frilly flowery ones!), washing line, BBQ, satellite dish... However, I am in need of a bed and a sofa to make it really habitable. Once I've got those, I think I'll really be settled in.

So anyone who's around the Southampton area at any time, please pop in and say hi! I have a couple of spare mugs and a working (if old and decrepit) kettle, so I can provide cups of tea and coffee; although if there's more than two of you you might have to bring your own chairs... I have two garden chairs and a bean bag to sit on at the moment!

Comments (1) Tuesday, 17th May, 2005, 17:16 - Snail's pace

I'm finding the house-buying process incredibly slow... I've signed a contract, got the mortgage offer, got the deposit ready, basically got everything in place that I can. I spoke to my solicitors last week, and we're now waiting to hear back from the seller with answers to a list of questions we put to him. Once we've got satisfactory answers (which very rarely happens first time apparently), we can then set dates for exchange etc.

It's been 8 weeks already, and I'm getting very nervous as I've got to be out of my flat by the end of May! Fingers crossed it will all go ok....!
