What's going on in Caroline's world...

Wednesday, 24th November || Unplugged

Well, things may have improved slightly - I phoned BT yesterday who said that in fact an appointment hadn't been booked for me and that December 14th was the earliest they could possibly do, would that suit? What? Eh? What happened to January 10th!? So I snapped that one up, naturally! However, transferring the account into my name from Kerry's is a whole other board game, which I'm not even going to tackle now, as that'll confuse the hell out of them and I'll never get a phone line!

It's amazing how disconnected you feel when you've got used to having wireless internet access at home: when it gets taken away and you don't have any way of accessing your e-mails except at work, and can't just pick up the laptop and check the tv guide, maybe do a bit of christmas shopping... you feel as if you've lost a limb or something. Bizarre. And slightly concerning.

Due to this ongoing lack-of-connection issue, I have some pictures waiting to be published which I can't upload, including one of a snowman called Simon! Let me explain... I went away for the weekend last weekend with some uni mates to Tewkesbury in Gloucestershire (or is it Worcestershire?). We hired a small cottage for the weekend, complete with exposed beams and real log fire! It was ace We went out for a walk on the Malvern hills on saturday and it started snowing! About 2 inches fell in about half an hour, so there we were, on top of a mountain which we'd climbed for the views and we could barely see each other. So obviously we built a snowman and had a snowball fight It was so much fun! Especially for Fiona, Helen's housemate from New Zealand, who really got a a taste of English life

Then we visited a Christmas Fayre in an old Priory, stopped at a 'local' pub for some grub (and found out that the landlord's daughter used to live just round the corner from my parents! Small world...), visited Upton-upon-Severn (which was looking quite flooded) and went back to the cottage as it got dark where we spent the evening playing board games. I actually won Trivial Pursuits! I don't think I've ever done that before! And Pictionary was sooo much fun...

Sunday we visited Tewkesbury with it's Abbey and lovely olde-worldy streets, had lunch at a lovely old pub and then journeyed down some lovely country lanes to get to Broadway Tower. This is a bizarre construction on top of a hill, inhabited by William Morris many years ago, from which you can look out over 13 of England's finest counties (on a good day) - we saw about 3 counties, lots of sheep and a lovely sunset!

So anyway, the pictures of this will be uploaded as soon as possible, probably along with any pictures from this weekend, which starts tonight with Mum & I driving up to York from where Dad & Steven are setting off early tomorrow on Day 1 of their 3 day Lombard Revival Rally expedition around the north of England. This is an event which has been organised to revive the 'spirit of the RAC rally' and harks back to bygone years of good old rallying (before the days of 4 wheel drive, traction control, stages which are over in a matter of minutes and which only happen during daylight hours). Mum & I are marshalling on the rally, which goes from York to Scarborough, the Yorkshire Moors & Dales, Carlisle, the Lakes, Chester and Wales, and finishes up in Cheltenham on Saturday evening. A total of 1400 miles in 3 days, in cars no bigger than 1.4 litres and at standard spec (except things which can be up-rated for safety reasons, e.g. brakes, suspension), and with no service crews. It should be great fun! And with people such as Gwyndaff Evans and Anthony Reid taking part, it should be a tough event. Good luck boys!

Right, back to work I suppose...
