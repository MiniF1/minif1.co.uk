Wednesday, June 18, 15:40 (Peru: BST -6:00) - Day 270
Cusco - post Machu Picchu
We are back from our Inca Trail all in one piece, with just a few aching muscles We set off by bus from Cusco on saturday morning, stopping in Ollyantaytambo to be beseiged by locals selling everything you could possibly want for the trek (Caroline managed to procure herself a walking stick for 40p) before starting the trek at the imaginatively named 'km 82'. We walked for 2 hours before reaching our first Inca ruin (Patallacta), possibly a gateway to Machu Picchu. Then it was up the valley to Wayllabamba for our first campsite.

The second day was the hardest trekking-wise - uphill all the way, over the 1st pass (4200m) then down to our second campsite. The third day was the most spectacular, with lots of ruins, lovely views and lots of nice wild flowers and hummingbirds along the way.

The fourth day we had to get up early (4am) to make it to Machu Picchu (Old Mountain) for sunrise (well we were supposed to stop at the Sun Gate but two of our group were being difficult and disappeared off so we had to follow them). The best view of Machu Picchu was from the Sun Gate, where you really appreciated the sheer size and unlikely location of it.

We spent the day being guided round the ruins, then Helen climbed Waynapicchu (Young Mountain) for superb views of the surrounding mountains and valleys while Caroline explored the ruins further.

Had a great time with a nice group of people - 2 Belgian girls, a Japanese guy and 2 Aussie girls - and great food throughout. We stayed overnight in Aguas Calientes (town down in the valley) and got the train/bus back to Cusco early this morning.

We have spent the day shopping to replace stuff we lost (we are now decked out in matching sunglasses) and chatting to people, including a guy we met on the boat down in Chile. It seems to be the start of some festival here at the moment, with loads of parades around the main plaza and a large food market selling 'Chiriuchi', a dish of guinea pig, chicken, pork, seaweed, caviar, veg. tortilla and corn - tasty
