Wednesday, March 05, 17:40 (New Zealand (DST): BST +12:00) - Day 165
Auckland - Rotorua
Have arrived safely in NZ and already seen loads. Spent a day looking round Auckland - climbing volcanos for good views, the normal museum as an intro to NZ and watched NZ lose the Americas Cup (sailing event) on a big screen in the harbour.

After having looked into the price of coach tickets we decided we had got used to the independence of our own transport and have hired a stationwagon (estate car!) for the duration of our stay here. Went for the big car so we could sleep in it - although it's not as luxurious as the campervan we have kitted ourselves out nicely (home made curtains and a small camping stove seem to be doing the job)! Unfortunately the first one was broken so after 400km we got a replacement driven down to us, both are automatics but you can't have everything!

From Auckland we drove through Kawakawa bay and Thames stopping in the Coromandel Forest Park for some scenic walks. We then went round the Coromandel Peninsula to Whitianga where we camped. Next day we visited Cook's beach and Shakespeare's Cliff before picking up our new car in Hahei then walking to Cathedral Cove (a giant arch eroded into the cliff) and stopping off at hotwater beach to build our own spa in the hot sand (ouch!).

Today we stopped at the Maclaren Falls on the way to Rotorua, NZ famous geothermal area, we spent the afternoon looking at geysers and bubbling mud pools and learning about Maori culture. This evening we are going to a traditional Moari dance and feast to complete the experience.

Have no definite plans for NZ yet although we have booked our Ferry between the Islands (Sun 9th out, Tues 25th back).
