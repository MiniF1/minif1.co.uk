Thursday, January 16, 14:05 (Australia (DST): BST +10:00) - Day 117
South Australia
We have now made it to Adelaide via the very scenic Great Ocean Road and lots more National Parks. A few of the places we stopped off at include Torquay, the Twelve Apostles (limestone stacks in the sea), Loch Ard Gorge (site of one of many shipwrecks along that coast), London Bridge (which has fallen down - well half of it collapsed!), a Grotto, Lorne (for fish & chips), Apollo Bay (for live music at the pub), Mount Eccles (an extinct volcano), Penola (an historic township), Naracoorte (stalagmite & fossil caves), Coroonga National Park (dried up halite lakes) and Adelaide.

In Adelaide we have visited the market and the art gallery, but more importantly the National Wine Centre where a very nice barman (John) gave us extra tastings of the wines... and then a few more! We're planning to stay here another day or two before heading on up for Ularu and Alice Springs, so don't panic if you don't hear from us for a little while!
