Monday, March 10, 15:00 (New Zealand (DST): BST +12:00) - Day 170
Rotorua - Kaikoura
We have switched islands now (the views from the ferry last night were pretty nice, although there's a lot of low cloud around at the moment) and are in the seaside town of Kaikoura, famous for its dolphins, whales and seals.

We had great fun at our Maori 'hangi' in Rotorua after we e-mailed last time - the compere managed to pick the one non-english-speaking person to go up on stage to be 'welcomed' by a maori guy, which involved much shouting and staring - the poor guy didn't know what was going on! The next day we visited the Museum of Rotorua (in an old baths building) and the Blue Baths (old thermal swimming pool) before heading off towards Taupo. On the way we went swimming in the geothermally heated Kerosene Creek (warmer than a hot bath!), walked round Craters of the Moon (a field with lots of steaming craters & mudpools), watched the sluice gates being opened on the Aratiatia Dam to flood some rapids (purely a show for the tourists!) and visited Huka Falls before reaching Lake Taupo (NZ's largest lake). On our journey out towards Napier that night we stopped to help a broken down car, which meant Helen negotiating a doberman & a bull mastiff (& a labrador) late at night to borrow a telephone to call the AA!

Napier is famous for its Art Deco architecture, so we did an historic walk around the city to admire it all. We also did a tannery tour to see sheepskins being turned into rugs & booties, and spent the afternoon on a 4x4 bus travelling o'er hill and down dale to a gannet colony. We spent 45 minutes up on the cliff within touching distance of these (rather large) birds trying to work out the difference between male & female!

On the way between here & Wellington we stopped at Mount Bruce Wildlife Sanctuary spotting rare NZ birds (inc kiwis), ducks, eels, tuatara (lizards) and learning about their habitats.

We stayed in the capital city for a day and a half, arriving saturday afternoon in time to visit the national museum (Te Papa). We then drove up Mount Victoria for nice views over the city and the bay, before hitting the pub to try more Kiwi beers and watch the rugby then going to the cinema to see the latest James Bond movie! Sunday we visited the Museum of Wellington City & Sea, took a tour of Parliament House, peered inside Old St Pauls cathedral and walked round the government district before getting the cable car up another hill and walking back down through the Botanic Gardens.

Then we caught the ferry to Picton in the South Island which arrived late at night (but did not have any coverage of the Grand Prix, much to our disgust!). In making lunch yesterday Helen managed to cut her thumb so that she couldn't drive and could therefore take full advantage of the wine tastings this morning at the Montana winery! She hopes it gets better quickly as we have booked on a sea-kayaking tour tomorrow to visit the seals!
