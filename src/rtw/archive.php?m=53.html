Sunday, February 09, 17:00 (Australia: BST +9:00) - Day 141
Hervey Bay
We left Townsville and headed south to Airlie beach where we climbed up Mount (hill) Rooper trying to get good views of the Whitsunday islands - unfortunately the early morning cloud decided to close in and all we could make out were silhouettes! We did manage to see a few snakes and a large Cane toad on the way so it was worth it! In the afternoon Caroline met up with Mark at the lagoon for a quick dip and to find out how he was doing - broke and without a car (dumped it in Brisbane) but having great fun and making loads of friends.

Risking the rain and windy roads we headed up into the hills for Eungella National Park - platypus spotting capitol of Aus. Having gatecrashed a guided night walk we spotted this nocturnal animal and then again the next morning when we explored the rainforest on our own. It was nice to see the rainforest in the right conditions - wet! (This was the driest Jan since records began) After an energetic morning we headed for the "low" area of the National park and Finch Hatton gorge to visit two nice waterfalls hidden in the rainforest, Arlauen falls and wheel of fire falls.

Exhausted and having had enough of the drizzle we headed towards Rockhampton for the Olsen Capricorn caves. The next morning we took a tour round the limestone caves complete with thousands of bats and an underground cathedral (supposedly better acoustics than the Sydney Opera house!). Next stop Koorana Crocodile farm where we met loads of crocs from 2days to approx 80years old with varying temperaments, far too many of the males ate their girlfriends for our liking though!

This morning we have visited Bundaberg Rum distillery for a tour of the factory and obviously took advantage of the free tasting session. You may be interested to know that their best selling product is Rum and Coke in a can hence their latest release - draught Rum and Coke! We tried all the varieties - normal (37%), liqueur, premixed with Ginger beer and other stuff as well as the draught. On the way to Hervey Bay we got stopped for a random breath test by the police and as they couldn't find a trace of alcohol in Helen we want our money back!

Tomorrow we are heading off to Fraser Island for a day tour of the worlds largest sand island after which we will carry on south.
